I CARE, INC.

Bridging Paths for Senior Mobility: Your Journey, Our Care

My role: User research, UI/UX design, Prototype & Validation

Team: Monisha Shukla, Haley Hetrick & Sara Howard

Timeline: 3 Weeks

THE CONTEXT

I CARE is a nonprofit that provides free transportation for fixed/low-income seniors throughout DeKalb County to medical appointments and grocery stores. I CARE provides this service with the help of volunteer drivers, who use their own vehicles and gas to transport clients, and who provide caring companionship in our door-to-door service.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Redesigning the website for the non-profit, I care ( Volunteer driver for Seniors). We fell in love with this organization's mission and felt they could use a website refresh as old design is unintuitive.

DESIGN PROCESS

Empathizing with the Users

USER RESEARCH

So the first step of our research was to identify who are the users for this non profit.

The main goal of the research was to find out what problem were the seniors facing to use this service and the problems faced by the volunteers to sign up for the service.

We aimed to Simplify the Senior Support Volunteer Engagement.

Proto persona for a volunteer : Jenn

PROTO PERSONA

We wanted to form a deeper understanding of our users' goals, needs, experiences, and behaviors.

We created our proto persona based on user interviews and surveys, and we kept updating it throughout the project as we gathered more data.

RESEARCH INSIGHTS

We wanted to know what motivated the user to become volunteers, what were the pain points and challenges faced by them when selecting an NGO / Volunteering, was the current website accessible to the target audience of seniors and the volunteers.

We started with User interview and Survey’s, to determine the common and basic pain points of volunteering.

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

In the Heuristic evaluation we found out these main issues which we wanted to address.

- The primary navigation needs to be more intuitive.

- The users are not able to identify the page they are on. Breadcrumb is missing.

- There is room for design improvement regarding button, fonts and image usage.

- Volunteer sign up process is not intuitive and volunteer can't schedule a shift due to lack of information.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

People interested in helping seniors within their community struggle to understand how to volunteer with I care due to lack of information about responsibilities, requirements and scheduling.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Before we made any major design decisions, we wanted to check out the competition and see what other options are available to the seniors we’re trying to serve. We reviewed 3 direct competitors Let’s Ride Atlanta , Senior Citizens, INC & Marta and 2 indirect competitors teledoc and Empower-line.

We were looking for non profit who help senior citizens get to there destination and see what other options are available to the seniors we’re trying to serve.
